US Shifts Policy on Ukraine Conflict

Wall Street Journal

2025/2/15

US troops will not be used to secure the peace in Ukraine following any ceasefire deal with Vladimir Putin, Donald Trump's new defence secretary has declared, as he signalled a dramatic shift in American foreign and defence policy away from Europe.

Pete Hegseth also said it is "unrealistic" to think Ukraine can return to its pre-2014 borders and he ruled out NATO membership as way to guarantee Kyiv's security.

This will be a huge blow for Volodymyr Zelenskyy. The interventions came at the opening of a meeting of allies in Brussels on Wednesday. He said: "We are not going to send American troops to fight in Ukraine. We are not going to send American troops to enforce a peace agreement in Ukraine."

The comments mark a significant departure from the policies of the previous administration, which had provided military aid to Ukraine and supported its aspirations to join NATO.
